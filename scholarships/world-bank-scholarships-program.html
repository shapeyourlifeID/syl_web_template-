World Bank Scholarships Program

Fully Funded for Postgraduate Master's Degree Program 24 universities in the U.S., Europe, Africa, Oceania and Japan

Mikael Toga
Published in Joint Japan/World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program | Policy | Dec 12, 2024

About

The Joint Japan/World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program is provided by the World Bank and is a fully funded program that offers the opportunity to pursue a Postgraduate Master’s degree. It is open to international students from a World Bank member developing country who intend to pursue master's studies at the 41 programs from the 24 universities provided by the World Bank.

Estimated Application Duration

13 January - 28 February

Benefits

  • Tuition

  • Living expenses depending on the host country (accommodations, food, books, etc.)

  • Economy class air travel between your home country and the host university at the start of your study program and immediately following the end of the scholarship period

  • USD $600 travel allowance during each of the pre-planned flights

  • Basic medical insurance

How to Apply

  • Be admitted to a participating program. The letter of admission must be uploaded before submitting the scholarship application.

  • Complete the Recommendation Request Form. This form requires the names and email addresses of two recommenders who can attest to the applicant’s professional qualifications.

  • Complete the online application form.

  • Submit the required documents. The application form requires you to upload scanned copies of documentation proving you meet the eligibility requirements, your bachelor's degree diploma, your CV, and your unconditional letter of admission.

  • If the applicant is selected as a finalist, they will be requested to submit a scanned copy of the identification page of their passport and a scanned copy of a health certificate.

Requirements

  • Two recommendation letters submitted online from professional contacts who can attest to the applicant’s qualifications

  • Completed Application Form, written in English or the language of the master's degree program.

  • Documentation that proves the applicant meets the eligibility requirements regarding their years of recent development-related work.

  • Bachelor’s degree diploma and, if applicable, the diploma of the applicant’s most advanced graduate degree

  • CV written in English or the language of the master’s degree program

  • Unconditional Letter of Admission to a JJ/WBGSP participating master's program

  • Completed Recommendation Request Form

  • Scanned copy of applicant’s passport's identification page or an equivalent legal document

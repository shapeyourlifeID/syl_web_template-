About

The Clarendon Fund provides more than 200 merit-based scholarships for graduate students at the University of Oxford, covering tuition and living costs. These scholarships are available to both part-time and full-time Master's and PhD students, aiming to give exceptional students the chance to pursue their studies and broaden their knowledge at Oxford. By attracting individuals from a wide range of backgrounds, the program creates an inclusive community that promotes collaboration across various disciplines and interests.

Estimated Application Duration

3 December or 7-8 January (depending on the specific course application deadline)

Benefits

  • Tuition fee coverage

  • Annual living expenses grant* (for full-time course scholars)

  • Study support grants* (for part-time students)

  • Compulsory course academic fees (for part-time courses with a modular fee structure, where the course does not have a fixed number of terms of fee liability)

*at least the UKRI minimum doctoral stipend rate (at least £18,622 for 2023-2024)

Eligibility Criteria

  • All applicants for graduate study at the University of Oxford are eligible

  • Applicants who hold a deferred graduate offer are not eligible to be considered for this scholarship

  • Current students who will continue to study for the same degree at Oxford in the next year are not eligible for this scholarship.

  • An excellent academic record is essential. The majority of successful scholars usually have at least a first-class honors degree or its equivalent.

  • The scholarship committee places major emphasis on an applicant’s future academic potential and aptitude for the proposed course of study

Note: Selection criteria may vary slightly depending on the subject area and whether applicants apply for a taught or research degree.

How to Apply

  • There is no separate procedure to apply for this scholarship.

  • The applicants must apply for graduate courses at Oxford University between December and January.

  • If the applicant fulfills the eligibility requirements, the college or institute automatically forwards the name of the application for the scholarship.

  • Application to your course will be considered by the academics working in your proposed field of study

  • Once you are shortlisted, the Clarendon Fund will contact you through email and will send you an offer.

Requirements

  • Online application

  • Reference letter

  • GRE General Test scores*

  • English Language Proficiency*

  • Official transcript and a translation of the transcript into English*

  • Statement of purpose and research proposal*

  • Other supporting documents*

* depends on the course*

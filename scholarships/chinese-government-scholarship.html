Chinese Government Scholarship Program

Fully Funded for Undergraduate, Postgraduate Master's, and Doctorate Degree Programs 279 universities in China

Mikael Toga
Published in Chinese Government Scholarship (CGS) | Policy | Dec 12, 2024

About

The Chinese Government Scholarship Program is provided by the Chinese Government and is a fully funded program that offers the opportunity to pursue an Undergraduate, Postgraduate Master’s, or Doctorate degree. It is open to international students from any country outside of the People’s Republic of China who intend to pursue undergraduate, master's or doctoral studies at any of the 274 partner universities.

Estimated Application Duration

15 December - 30 April

Benefits

  • Tuition fee coverage

  • Free university dormitory or accommodation subsidy

  • Stipend per month
    a. Undergraduates: CNY 2,500
    b. Master’s students: CNY 3,000
    c. Doctoral students: CNY 3,500

  • Comprehensive medical insurance of CNY 800 per year;

Eligibility Criteria

  • Be a citizen of a country other than the People’s Republic of China

  • Be in good health both mentally and physically

  • Have a high school diploma or its equivalent when applying for an undergraduate program

  • Have a Bachelor’s degree when applying for a Master’s program

  • Have a Master’s degree when applying for a Doctoral program

  • Pass the HSK (Level 3) when applying for a Chinese-taught undergraduate, general, or senior scholar program

  • Pass the HSK (Level 4) when applying for a Chinese-taught postgraduate program

  • Meet the requirements of language proficiency and academic ability when applying for a program taught in a language other than Chinese.

How to Apply

  • Apply to the designated university

  • Request a recommendation from one of the partner organizations

  • Register for an account to fill out the online Application Form

  • Fill out the online Application Form

  • The China Scholarship Council (CSC) will notify the admission results to the application receiving agency, which will subsequently inform the applicant.

Requirements

  • Application Form for Chinese Government Scholarship (filled in online);

  • A scanned copy of the Passport Information Page

  • Diploma and certificate of highest education (notarized photocopy)

  • Academic transcripts in either Chinese or English;

  • Certificate of language proficiency. (As required by each respective university)

  • Certificate of Non-Criminal Record

  • Post-Graduate only:
    a. University Admission documents
    b. A study or research plan written in Chinese or English
    c. Two recommendation letters in Chinese or English from professors or associate professors

  • Additional documents:
    a. Art or design portfolio (Any applicants applying for arts or design program)
    b. Legal documents of legal guardians in China (Any applicants under the age of 18)
